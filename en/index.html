MINO. — A culinary time capsule where Armenian and French heritage collide

Brand Identity, Product Design
Mino is a culinary bridge between Armenia’s smoky hearths and French’ refined tables—a high-end dining experience where hand-chopped lamb tartare meets lavash ash brittle, and Wagyu glazed in pomegranate molasses crackles over open flame.
The brand identity mirrors this duality: a marriage of rugged textures and delicate artistry, with typography carved like Armenian stone scripts and visuals glowing like embers under cast-iron. Here, every dish is a relic, every bite a whispered history.
The Armenian guardian dog meets his equal, French cuisine.

VERBEEK — A visual identity for modern craftsmanship

Brand Identity, Logo Design
For Verbeek Interieur, a carpenter based near Leuven, I developed a visual identity that reflects their dedication to craftsmanship, precision, and modern aesthetics. The result is a timeless design that balances strength and elegance. The visual identity was expanded through a full set of logo variations, a color palette rooted in white and a rich blue, and a detailed brand guide to ensure consistency across print and digital media.

KU LEUVEN — Designing interactive tools to map Flanders’ cultural heritage landscape

UX/UI Design, Data Visualization
KU Leuven’s Department of Cultural Studies commissioned me as Lead UX/UI Designer to support a major research project on the state of cultural heritage in Flanders. I designed interactive data visualizations: some showing clusters of organizations based on discipline, and others mapping their relationships through node-based networks.
The project also included a printed research dossier with clear typography, a refined color palette, and custom graphs to present complex findings in an accessible way. These tools now help the Flemish government make informed decisions around funding, policy, and preservation strategies.
